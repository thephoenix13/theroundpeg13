You
Why is query #4471 taking 2.3 seconds? It used to be under 100ms.
PGF Pal
Query #4471 is performing a sequential scan on the orders table (4.2M rows). This scan cost jumped from 0.3 to 2.3s as the table grew past 3M rows last week.
-- Suggested fix (safe to run on live cluster): CREATE INDEX CONCURRENTLY idx_orders_customer ON orders(customer_id); -- Expected impact: ~73% cost reduction -- Est. build time: ~4 minutes
I'll monitor after deployment and alert you if latency doesn't drop within 10 minutes.
You
Safe to run during business hours?
PGF Pal
Yes — CONCURRENTLY builds without locking the table. Current connection pool is at 42%, so no risk of contention. Proceed when ready.

Common Questions

Straight answers to real objections.

We already have a DBA. Why Postgres 1st? +
Your DBA is valuable — and they need backup. We cover after-hours incidents, specialist deep-dives, and second opinions on architecture. Most clients say their DBA's productivity increases because we handle reactive firefighting.
Why not just use AWS RDS or managed Postgres? +
RDS gives you infrastructure. It doesn't give you engineers who understand your query patterns, tune your indexes, or diagnose your specific regression at 3AM. We complement cloud Postgres — most of our clients run on AWS or GCP.
How risky is the Oracle or MSSQL migration? +
Every migration carries risk — and we're honest about that. Our process starts with a full assessment, we plan for rollback scenarios, and we execute during low-traffic windows with monitoring in place throughout.
Can we start with just monitoring before committing? +
Yes. PGF WatchTower is available standalone. Many clients start there, see the insights, get comfortable with our team, and expand from there. No pressure to commit upfront.
What makes you different from EDB or Percona? +
EDB and Percona are US-based, priced in dollars, and built for global enterprise deals. We're India-based, IST-aligned, priced in rupees, and built for the Indian market — without cutting corners on expertise.
