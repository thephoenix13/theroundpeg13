The difference

Tools answer in one shot. Agents do the whole job. — How to think about the catalogue

Tools are prompt-based, single-task. You give them an input — a JD, a transcript, a contract — and get a structured output. Useful as embedded utilities or as on-demand productivity boosters.

Agents are multi-step. They research, decide, act across multiple touchpoints — calling APIs, browsing sources, holding multi-turn conversations. Useful when the work is a workflow, not a query.

We help clients deploy either, mix and match, or build new ones from scratch. Available as standalone integrations or as part of a broader Round Peg 13 engagement.
